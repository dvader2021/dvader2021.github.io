The Green Card Diary - Part 2

10 minutes read   published May 2022

The campus life

I'm here in this quaint town of Kingston. Quiet.. dead silence .. there are no people for miles around .. the mind struggles as it compares to the cacophony of noise in India.

My first day is a disaster .. I'm jet lagged and confused with the time zones .. I walk into the kitchen and look at the mess that is in. Cyrus walks by with his cup of coffee. I'm left staring .. I have to make my own coffee.

A week has gone by .. I have not had any good food as yet .. good food still means rice with curry. I have been struggling to cook. Adhi and Sheila, the other newcomers, have put in their effort but we have had no success. The food that we cook is barely edible. Yet the stress of the new country, the search for a part time job, an apartment to stay and just about everything has minimized the food situation. Milk, cereal and bread comes to the rescue as always.

Soon I settle into a new daily rhythm .. classes during day time and work early morning and late night.

I take up a job in the cafeteria. I hate cleaning dishes but this is the only one available and it pays .. My ego looks down on me but my finances wants me to work .. My pride will not let me ask my family for money so here I'm working two shifts to earn $5.50 an hour .. At least I can take care of my bills.  

The culture shock

Late night, I walk into pizza hut for the first time with a group of friends .. all vegetarians, newly arrived as students .. we order a vegetarian pizza with tomatoes, onions, peppers and olives. A few minutes later the waitress walks back to the table and says "sorry guys, we are out of tomatoes. May I substitute with Pepperoni”. Clueless .. we look at each other .. and just nod our heads.

And what a delicious pizza it was .. Only later, we found what pepperoni actually was. Sandeep, a staunch vegetarian, cried for a whole week to atone the “sin” that he committed by eating meat.

Another first .. A visit to McDonalds with Patel.. Patel, a vegetarian stood in the line and when his turn came in, spoke with an attitude and a pronounced accent "a double cheese hamburger without any cheese and meat". The employee scratched her head and then after a brief pause, gave him two slices of the bread - double cheese hamburger with no meat and cheese. Those were the days when the condiments were outside and you helped yourself to it.

Snow and wonderland

The day of a great blizzard .. Snow, pure white snow for miles and wind howling at top speed .. After the blizzard, I run and frolic and jump on the snow .. eating some of that too. An hour later, I'm exhausted and cold.

On another deserted cold day .. I’m driving home with Chandra from the Engineering lab. We see miles of white snow, decorated houses and a deserted empty town. As we  turn round the corner,  we see a lone guy running .. jogging. Without any further thought, Chandra brings the car to halt in line with the running guy, opens the windows and in a thick Indian accent says “do you need a ride back home?"  The running guy briefly paused, looked at us and continued running. His stern look said everything and we hurried away.

Settling Down

The initial euphoria is gone. I'm bored, desperate. I have no real job, no money, I m not doing well in school, the weather is extremely cold. I’m slowly dropping into depression. I wake up with thoughts of killing myself. I long for the beautiful sunshine in Bombay and the hot food back home. I hate everything in America. Is this what I dreamed of all my life? The cold is playing havoc with my thoughts.   And the cold and loneliness is wreaking havoc with others too .. pushing into doing all kinds of things. Pawan is into meditation and invites some strange guys and meditates for hours together. Raghav is into cooking .. inviting other students to visit and eat with him .. many declining the invitation after the first time. Shanti was int shopping. She had her cache of admirers and they followed her everywhere like a little puppy. And there was Suresh Reddy .. one lone ranger who wore his long winter coat – rain, shine or summer. We could smell him from miles as he walked around the campus.

Summer dawns. I get a paid internship. Life is suddenly back on track. Money is slowly flowing in. I can indulge .. at least couple of cups of coffee at the local coffee shop .. I’m elated.

The Girl

I see her for the first time at a party in a friends place .. tall, long blonde hair and deep cut marks on one side of her cheek .. unusual but pretty .. I stood staring at her not knowing how to talk. She looked at me inquisitively and I quickly scurried away. Next time, I see her hard at work at a local restaurant. She looked fabulous in her Jeans and black sweater .. still could not muster any courage to talk to her.

A week down, I run into her again. She smiles and introduces herself. We become friends .. good friends.

As I lay on my bed and reminisce about the past year, I remember my initial guide saying At first, you will love the new culture, the environment (everything's new); Then as you settle down, you will hate everything (you miss home); At last you will learn to reconcile to accept this life (you make the compromise)

The name game

I heard someone calling .. was it my name? I don’t know when but my name has been butchered, authored, shaped and gone through all possible permutations. I have learnt to accept it as part of the journey. Initially I struggled with the misspellings but then after countless tries in correcting .. I’m used to it.   It was difficult not to chuckle at the plight of other fellow Indian names being butchered. Krishnaswamy Paliniswamy was a mouthful even for me although he preferred Kris as his given name. And there was Kulbhushan Singh Karbhanda. Many will not even try saying the name but few daredevils try .. rolling their eyes and tongue .. saying some form which vaguely resembles the name .. and walking away feeling in seventh heaven. The girls on the other hand will not stop giggling when they hear these names .. leaves me thinking if you cant have them say your name, what chance do you have dating them?   And sometimes you find the irony on this side. Take Muthukrishnan Swaminathan for instance. He diligently spells his name at every given chance - M U T H U K  R I S H N A N   S W A M I N A T H A N, at times going to the extent of saying M as in Monkey U as Umbrella T as in Tom... 

The Green Card

Few years later .. Graduated .. I have a decent, well paying job and my application for Green card is being processed. Life is all fun now.

Finally, here it is. I hold the letter from the Immigration department.. nervous … with trembling hands, I open it .. stuck on a white letter, folded in threes, a simple square card.

I'm now officially a Green Card holder. My new journey begins today. The feeling of achievement cannot be explained ..
